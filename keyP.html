Oklohoma, Route 66 | Key Points

Overall Key Factors

Economic Struggles: The migration was a response to severe economic hardship caused by the Great Depression and exacerbated by the Dust Bowl.

Route 66 Symbolism: Route 66 became a symbol of the American Dream and the struggles faced by those seeking a better life.

Social Impact: The Okie migration had a lasting impact on the cultural and social fabric of the regions along Route 66, shaping the narrative of American migration during a pivotal period in history.
